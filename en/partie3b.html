How was the Gwangju uprising influenced by the development of new forms of image-bearing media?

Focus

The Gwangju uprising is also profoundly influenced by the evolution of new forms of image-bearing media. These media have shaped the perception and commemoration of this event, as well.

May 1980 in Gwangju

Social networks

Social networks are helping to pass on the memory of historical events. Platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, Instagram and YouTube allow users to share memories, photos, videos and articles about the Gwangju uprising. They facilitate the rapid and widespread transmission of information, enabling wider awareness and discussion. Hashtags, commemorative videos and personal testimonies create an online community of committed people. Hashtags like #GwangjuUprising help to keep the remembrance in the public consciousness.

Websites and Digital Archives

The Gwangju Democracy Museum website provides access to a wealth of historical resources, including photographs, videos, eyewitness accounts and official documents. The digital archive thus enables in-depth research and individual exploration of the event, facilitating self-directed learning and personal discovery of history. New forms include cyber-pilgrimage, with websites such as cyber.518.org providing a new form of celebration and testimony for the relatives of the victims. Through digital tombs and comments sections specifically dedicated to them, these spaces allow several needs to converge: mourning, preserving memory and passing it on.

Audiovisual

The 2017 movie ‘A Taxi Driver’ breathes new life into the view of the uprising. This movie tells the story of a taxi driver who helps a foreign journalist who is documenting the events. It has played an important role in raising public awareness, particularly among the younger generation, by providing a dramatic and emotional portrayal of the events. In fact, movies and documentaries often provide a human and visual perspective that helps viewers to understand and empathize with historical events. They make history accessible and memorable, often reaching a wider audience than written texts alone. For some people less exposed to it, familiarity with the uprising comes only through media such as kpop music videos, webtoon or film.

Virtual reality, augmented reality and artificial intelligence

Educational projects using VR and AR recreate the events of Gwangju, allowing users to experience an immersive simulation of this historic moment. These technologies make history more interactive and engaging, especially for younger generations. They offer a unique educational experience that can deepen understanding and empathy.
The rise of artificial intelligence offers many opportunities to enrich the way young Koreans process historical images, but it can also contribute to misinformation. On the one hand, by improving access to, understanding of and interaction with these images, A.I can help preserve the collective memory and educate future generations about crucial events such as the Gwangju uprising. On the other hand, the manipulation of images, with phenomena such as the proliferation of faked images and deep fakes, means that new generations are distancing themselves from images, which may no longer appear to be a reliable source of information about the past and may therefore be contributing to the disinterest of young Koreans.
The evolution of new forms of media, including film, social media, virtual reality, and digital archives, is playing a key role in commemorating and understanding the Gwangju Uprising. These media make history more accessible, engaging and relevant to younger generations, ensuring that the event remains alive in the collective memory. Education, active participation in commemorations, and the influence of popular culture also plays their part. This event continues to serve as a powerful symbol for the ongoing struggles for democracy and human rights in South Korea.
