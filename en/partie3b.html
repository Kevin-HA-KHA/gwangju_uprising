How was the Gwangju uprising influenced by the development of new forms of image-bearing media?

Focus

The Gwangju uprising is also profoundly influenced by the evolution of new forms of image-bearing media. These media have shaped the perception and commemoration of this event, as well.

May 1980 in Gwangju

Social networks

Beyond print and digital media, social networks play a significant role in preserving and transmitting memory. Platforms like Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and YouTube enable users to share memories, photos, videos, and articles about the Gwangju Uprising. They facilitate the rapid and widespread dissemination of information, allowing for broader awareness and discussion. Hashtags, commemorative videos, and personal testimonies create an online community of people committed to preserving historical memory. Hashtags like #GwangjuUprising keep the event alive in the public consciousness.

Websites and digital archives

The website of the Gwangju Democracy Museum provides access to a wealth of historical resources, including photographs, videos, testimonies, and official documents. Digital archives allow for in-depth research and individual exploration of the event, facilitating self-directed learning and personal discovery of history. The concept of "cyber-pilgrimage" has also emerged, where sites like cyber.518.org offer a new form of commemoration and testimony for the relatives of victims. Through digital graves and dedicated comment spaces, these platforms address multiple needs: mourning, memory preservation, and its transmission.

Audiovisual

The 2017 film A Taxi Driver brought renewed attention to the Gwangju Uprising. The film tells the story of a taxi driver who helps a foreign journalist document the events, playing a significant role in raising public awareness, especially among younger generations, by providing a dramatic and emotional portrayal of the events. Films and documentaries offer a human and visual perspective that helps viewers understand and empathize with historical events. They make history accessible and memorable, often reaching a broader audience than written texts alone. For some, familiarity with the uprising comes solely through media such as K-pop music videos, webtoons, or films.

Virtual reality, augmented reality and artificial intelligence

Educational projects using VR and AR recreate the events of Gwangju, allowing users to experience an immersive simulation of this historical moment. These technologies make history more interactive and engaging, particularly for younger generations. They offer a unique educational experience that can deepen understanding and empathy.
The rise of AI offers numerous opportunities to enrich how Korean youth engage with historical images, but it also presents risks of misinformation. On the one hand, by improving access, understanding, and interaction with these images, AI can help preserve collective memory and educate future generations about crucial events like the Gwangju Uprising. On the other hand, with the proliferation of doctored images and deepfakes, younger generations may become more skeptical of the authenticity of images, potentially leading to a disengagement from historical events.
The evolution of new forms of media, including films, social media, virtual reality, and digital archives, plays a crucial role in the commemoration and understanding of the Gwangju Uprising. These media make history more accessible, engaging, and relevant to younger generations, ensuring that the event remains alive in collective memory. Education, active participation in commemorations, and the influence of popular culture also play key roles. This event continues to serve as a powerful symbol for ongoing struggles for democracy and human rights in South Korea.
