How do young people perceive the Gwangju uprising today ?

Focus

The Gwangju Uprising, also known as the Gwangju Uprising or the Gwangju Democratic Movement, is a major historical event in South Korea. The perception of this event by young Koreans today may vary, but several general trends emerge.

May 1980 in Gwangju

Activism and Heritage, Historical Awareness and Education

The Gwangju movement is covered in history lessons and discussions on democracy as well as human rights in South Korea. For the younger part of the population, this event still inspires activism and political commitment. They see a direct link between past struggles and current societal challenges, using this legacy as a basis for promoting human rights and democracy.

This perception inspires a sense of national pride among young Koreans, who see the event as a testament to the determination and courage of their elders. For others, the Gwangju uprising is seen as a distant memory which they feel no connection to, despite the commemorations of the Gwangju uprising being held and the press proving that interest is still high.
